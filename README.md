## Value Proposition (What you get)
I build **manufacturing-grade ERP/CRM workflows** that reduce operational friction and make data reliable — without turning teams into “software operators”.

**Core delivery themes**
- **Quote-to-Cash (Q2C):** pricing rules → quote versioning → approvals → order handoff → invoicing alignment  
- **Procure-to-Pay (P2P):** requisitions → RFQs → supplier comparison → approvals → PO → GRN → invoice matching  
- **Inventory Accuracy:** traceability, controlled adjustments, cycle counting programs, multi-warehouse transfers  
- **CRM ↔ ERP Integration:** stable contracts, idempotency, retries, audit trail, observability  
- **API-first Architecture:** REST/Webhooks for accounting, logistics, marketplaces, and internal systems

**Typical outcomes**
- Faster approvals, fewer “stuck” purchase cycles  
- Higher stock accuracy & traceability (who/when/why)  
- Integrations that don’t break every month  
- Dashboards that drive actions (not vanity metrics)

## Case Study (Representative Engagement)
> **Note:** The details below are generalized to protect client confidentiality.

### Context
A manufacturing/distribution team ran critical workflows across spreadsheets + disconnected tools:
- RFQs and supplier comparisons in email threads  
- Inventory adjustments without clear reasons or approvals  
- Pricing exceptions handled manually (margin leakage)  
- Integrations failing silently (rework + delayed deliveries)

### Approach
- **Workflow audit** (current-state map + bottleneck list + quick-win plan)
- Implemented **governed workflows** (roles, approvals, exception handling, audit trails)
- Built **API-first integrations** (idempotency, retries, structured logging, alerts)
- Introduced **data governance rules** for master data + change history
- Added **operational dashboards** (cycle time, variance signals, compliance metrics)

### Results (Operational Outcomes)
- Procurement cycle time reduced by removing manual handoffs and approval chasing
- Inventory variance became traceable (who/when/why) with controlled adjustments
- Pricing exceptions moved to governed approvals, improving margin control
- Integrations stabilized with monitoring and replay-safe delivery

### What made it work
- Clear ownership per workflow step (business + IT)
- Smaller set of non-negotiable rules (instead of “more features”)
- Reliable integrations treated as production infrastructure
